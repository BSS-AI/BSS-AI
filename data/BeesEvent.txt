{"text": "{{DISPLAYTITLE:Event Bees}}
        '''Event Bees''' are [[bees]] that can only be obtained by purchasing/acquiring their
        [[Egg#Event Bee Eggs|specific eggs]]. They are generally very expensive, costing either
        Robux, [[ticket]]s, or are time-consuming to acquire, requiring completion of a long series
        of [[quests]] or collection of [[items]]. Some could have been purchased as a [[First
        Edition Bee]], which are bees with a \"1st\" flag on top of them. The flags serve as decor and
        don't actually affect anything about the bee, as they perform the same compared to a
        non-First Edition bee. Event bees have unique [[Ability Tokens|abilities]] that are not seen
        in any other type of bee and appear with a green background on the [[Hive Slot|hive slot]]
        they are in. There are currently a total of 11 Event bees, composed of 7
        [[:Category:Colorless|colorless]], 2 [[:Category:Red|red]], and 2 [[:Category:Blue|blue]].
        Event Bees are a one-time purchase.

        When hatching an Event bee egg, the egg turns into a special [[Royal Jelly#Royal Jelly
        Variants|royal jelly]] that will always transform a bee into that specific Event bee. Since
        a hive can only contain one of any given Event bee, the special royal jelly only exists for
        the purpose of recovering the Event bee if the player transformed it.

        There is a strategy that takes advantage of the special Event bee jelly to essentially
        switch the [[bond]] of two bees around. For example, if the player had a level 1 [[Cobalt
        Bee]] and a level 7 bee, such as a [[Hasty Bee]], the player could use a royal jelly on the
        Cobalt Bee. This would make the special royal jelly available. If they use the special jelly
        on the Hasty Bee, the player would now have a level 7 Cobalt Bee and a random level 1 bee.
        All Event bees can become gifted, though none have a favorite treat. Giving an Event bee a
        [[Star Treat|Star Treat]] has a 100% chance to turn the bee gifted, and a [[Gingerbread
        Bear]] has a 1% chance of turning the bee gifted. Those currently are the only ways to get a
        gifted Event bee (with the exception of the first edition [[Bear Bee]], which is
        automatically gifted), mentioned by quest-giving [[Onett]] past the [[30 Bee Gate]], as well
        as [[Panda Bear]] towards the end of his questline. Note that once an Event bee becomes
        gifted, its Event bee royal jelly becomes basically useless, since using a royal jelly on a
        gifted bee will make it lose its gifted status.

        == Trivia ==
        *This and the [[Bees/Common|Common]] rarity are the only bee rarities that cannot be
        obtained through normal royal jellies.
        *This rarity has the least ways of getting a bee of this rarity with only two ways which are
        Event bee eggs and Event bee jellies.
        *Bear Bee, Digital Bee, Gummy Bee, Vicious Bee, and Windy Bee are the only event bees that
        aren't obtained by tickets.
        **Prior to the [[Updates#2019.04.05|04/05/19 update]], Gummy Bee was obtainable by tickets.
        *The audio that plays when an Event bee notification pops up is the same audio when a
        [[Bees/Legendary|Legendary bee]] notification pops up.
        * Bear Bee and Gummy Bee are the only Event bees that don't have an ability that directly
        collects pollen.
        *Digital Bee & Bear Bee are the only Event bees to have a gifted ability.
        *Every Event Bee has 1-3 Ability Tokens that is unique to them. Event Bees share this trait
        with [[Bees/Mythic|Mythic Bees]].
        *The Event bees rarity is tied with the Epic bees rarity for having the most bees, being 11.
