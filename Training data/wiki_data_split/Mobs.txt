{"text": "There are 34 different kinds of '''enemies/creatures/monsters''' (colloquially called
        “'''mobs'''”) in [[Bee Swarm Simulator]].

        Other than [[ants]] and [[Robo Bear Challenge]] mobs, the most frequently occurring mob is
        the [[Rhino Beetle|rhino beetle]] as there are five of them in the game with [[ladybug]]s
        being the second with four. The least frequently occurring mob is the [[Stump Snail]],
        respawning in 96 hours (4 days) or 81 hours and 36 minutes or 3.4 days (if the player owns a
        [[Gifted Bee|Gifted]] [[Vicious Bee]]) upon defeat.

        The 8 entities that can attack other mobs are [[bees]], [[flame]]s, [[frog]]s, [[Rogue
        Vicious Bee]], [[Wild Windy Bee]], [[Ability Tokens|abilities]], [[Passive
        Abilities|passives]], [[coconut]]s, and the coal summoned from the [[Naughty List]] during
        [[:Category:Beesmas|Beesmas]]. One method for defeating mobs that reliably works for any mob
        that defends a [[field]] (everything other than the [[King Beetle]], [[Tunnel Bear]], [[Cave
        Monster|cave monsters]], [[Stick Bug]], Rogue Vicious Bee, Wild Windy Bee, and the
        [[Chicks#Commando Chick|Commando Chick]]), with the exception of the [[Coconut Crab]] and
        [[Chicks#Mondo Chick|Mondo Chick]], is to jump continuously. It is one of the [[glitches]]
        where when jumping, mobs will freeze and not attack which leads to the player’s bees
        thinking the mob isn't a threat and not attack it. Players would have to make sure their
        bees \"know\" to attack a mob before starting to jump - if the mob has not started attacking
        them - by waiting for their bees to begin attacking the mob before jumping.

        Before the [[Updates#2019.02.01|2/1/2019 update]], rewards from defeating any mob were sent
        to the player's inventory right after the mob was defeated. After the update, the mobs were
        altered to drop a ring of tokens around it instead ([[Battle Points|battle points]] do not
        spawn as tokens). Other players cannot collect tokens dropped by the mob if the mob was not
        spawned by them. The tokens can be collected by a [[Ability Tokens#Token Link|token link]],
        unless the rewards are from public mobs, meaning other players can collect the drops
        regardless of whether they attacked it or not.

        Mobs have levels that are shown next to their name. If a player’s bees are at a lower level
        than the mob they are attacking, they have a higher chance of missing their attacks
        depending on how far the level of the bee is from the level of the mob being attacked. This
        means that the lower the level of the bee, the more likely a mob that has a higher level
        than the bee attacking will deflect or dodge the bee's attacks.

        Some mobs are considered passive. This means that they will not attack the player, nor the
        player will attack them. These include [[Bean Bugs|bean bugs]], [[frog]]s, [[fireflies]],
        with the exception of [[Chicks#Chick|Chicks]], [[Chicks#Spotted Chick|Spotted Chicks]], and
        [[Chicks#Hostage Chick|Hostage Chicks]].

        While some mobs are considered [[:Category:Bosses|bosses]], others do not qualify for this
        designation. These mobs are called [[:Category:Mini Bosses|mini-bosses]], distinct from
        regular mobs but not qualifying as a boss. [[Wild Windy Bee]], [[Rogue Vicious Bee]],
        [[Snowbear]], Commando Chick, and Stump Snail are all in this category.

        Daylight Saving Time will affect the respawn time of the Stump Snail, King Beetle, and
        Tunnel Bear.

        [[Loot Luck]] has the ability to directly affect the quantity and quality of the rewards
        that mobs drop upon defeat, but will not affect honey dropped by mobs (that is instead
        affected by [[System Page#Honey From Tokens|Honey from Tokens]].)

        When the Mondo Chick and Commando Chick use their egg block ability, it will often block the
        bee attack, even if the bee is the same level or a higher level than it.

        Stump Snail has the highest health out of all mobs and does not intentionally attack the
        player. Instead, it moves in a circle around the edge of the [[Stump Field]]. However, it
        will deal damage if the player stands in the front of the snail or in its head. Standing
        inside the shell will not deal any damage to the player.

        From 7/20/22 to 7/29/22, there was a Roblox bug that caused mobs to not work properly.

        == Regular Mobs==
        Regular mobs stay in certain fields and will attack the player if they enter that field.
        Once defeated, they will respawn after a period of time. Monster Respawn Time can affect how
        often they respawn.
